## Misc
Undergraduate Research Scholar and Distinguished Undergraduate Research Scholar 

The Undergraduate Research Scholar and Distinguished Undergraduate Research Scholar distinctions serve to recognize undergraduate accomplishments in research and encourage students to disseminate their research beyond the UNCW community. Scholars will receive commemorative items to wear during graduation and will be announced at the Student Showcase and on social media.

Note that these distinctions are only awarded to graduating seniors so you must have already filed for graduation. You will additionally need to provide proof (e.g., screenshots) for your achievements. However, you can use activities from your entire four years to satisfy the requirements.

There are three categories involved with these distinctions. To receive the Undergraduate Research Scholar distinction, you need to have completed Categories I and II. To receive the Distinguished Undergraduate Research Scholar distinction, you must have completed Categories I and III. More details can be found within the application. Students can achieve either one of the distinctions or both.

- Category I: Complete 6 credit hours of 291, 491, 499, or have worked the equivalent number of hours for pay.
- Category II: Present at a conference external to UNCW OR submit your research to an undergraduate research journal (does not need to have been accepted)
- Category III: Submission of research to professional or peer-reviewed journal (does not need to have been accepted); submission of application to a National Fellowship (list of eligible fellowships is within the application; do not need to have been awarded the Fellowship); successful application for a grant or award of more than $500 from a source external to UNCW; had a performance or juried exhibit with significant exposure; received award of outstanding research granted from external organization (e.g., best poster award).

Preferred Deadline: April 20th 2026; Final Deadline: May 3 2026
